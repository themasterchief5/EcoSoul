How Tree Planting Can Reverse Climate Change

By Anna Green | April 10, 2025

Tree planting isn't just symbolic — it's one of the most effective tools in our fight against climate change.

Reforestation has the potential to capture up to 25% of global annual carbon emissions. By restoring degraded forests and planting in deforested areas, we promote biodiversity, water retention, and climate stability. In regions like the Amazon or Borneo, aggressive reforestation has already shown measurable positive climate impact. Trees act as carbon sinks, filter water, and restore habitats for countless species.

Top 5 Native Trees to Plant in Your Region

By Mark Woods | March 28, 2025

Discover native trees that are best suited for your local climate and soil conditions.

  • European Beech – retains moisture and supports rich forest ecosystems.
  • Scots Pine – resilient to dry, poor soils.
  • Pedunculate Oak – home to over 400 species of insects.
  • Silver Birch – quick to grow and excellent for rewilding.
  • Small-leaved Lime – supports pollinators and offers dense canopy shade.

Choosing native trees enhances biodiversity and reduces the risk of disease and invasive species issues.

How Forests Improve Mental Health

By James Leaf | Feb 22, 2025

Nature immersion improves well-being, reduces stress, and boosts mood.

Research shows that just 20 minutes spent in a forest reduces cortisol levels and improves memory. Forest therapy, also known as Shinrin-yoku in Japan, is gaining traction globally. Reconnecting with nature has become a vital tool in treating burnout and anxiety.
